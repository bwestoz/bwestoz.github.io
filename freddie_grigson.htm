Freddie Grigson began his musical career in Melbourne but moved to Perth early in the 1980s. His artistic and technical virtuosity is well known having worked with greats including Sammy Davis Jnr, Joe Pass, Herb Ellis, Billy Cobham, Anthony Wilson, Mike Stern, Shirley Bassey, Jim McNeely, Dale Barlow and Don Burrows. He has collaborated with jazz guitar giants including Jim Hall, John Abercrombie, Joe Diorio and John Schofield. He is one of Australia's most in demand guitarists having been part of the Australian jazz scene for over 35 years and currently holds the position of head of Jazz Guitar studies at the West Australian Academy of Performing Arts (WAAPA).
